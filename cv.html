Bio

I am a researcher, lecturer and theorist with a PhD in Cultural Studies from Goldsmiths University of London. I remain affiliated to Goldsmiths as a Graduate School Fellow, and hold the position of Researcher at the Autonomy Institute – a progressive policy research organisation. I have almost a decade of professional writing experience, spanning commercial, policy and academic forms.

As an academic researcher, my work falls primarily in the fields of software studies and media studies, with a particular interest in networked communication technologies. My PhD posed an interdisciplinary investigation of two pivotal information processes – compression and encryption – drawing links between the technical, the cultural, the political and the economic.

In the field of public policy, I research and develop proposals for the future of work and universal basic services (UBS). I develop and pilot innovative solutions to expand the possibilities of public provision on a common or universal basis.

Professional Experience

  • 2021 - 2025 Goldsmiths, University of London - Associate Lecturer
    I have four years’ experience teaching in cultural studies, media studies and film studies, focusing on digital technology, media theory, critical theory and cultural politics. My practice is informed by critical pedagogies, notably that of Stuart Hall, by situationist and by liberationist approaches.
    At Goldsmiths, I designed and delivered student-focused seminars and lectures based on my own research, including small and large group teaching, and designed and marked assessments.
  • 2023 - Present The Autonomy Institute - Policy Researcher
    My work includes coordinating, building evidence for and developing innovative policy proposals for work and public futures, with particular emphasis on working time reduction and expanding public services – including shifting public perceptions of what is possible. I also design and facilitate piloting of radical policies for local authorities and employers and run policy-to-impact workshops for academics to think, express and promote their research in terms of public policy.
    This work is informed by theoretical approaches – notably drawing on post-work, feminism, ecology and systems theory – as well as the latest evidence from academic research, and in some cases by my own academic work.
  • 2019 - 2021 Top10VPN - VPN Researcher
    In this role I carried tech journalism (including test design) focused on consumer advocacy for ‘digital privacy’ and ‘security’. It was an SEO-first operation, and I developed a strong working knowledge of best practice, in content as well as web design and user experience.
  • 2018 - 2019 Health & Care - Copywriter
    Commercial copywriting for an online retailer, spanning a range of products, shopfronts and both consumer and business markets.
