Liam Mullally

I'm an interdisciplinary researcher and writer who focuses on, in one form or another, materialist media studies, information infrastructures and technologies of socialisation.

I work between a few knowledge-industries: today I am a PhD student and an associate lecturer at Goldsmiths, University of London, and a researcher at the Autonomy Institute. I've previously worked as a journalist and, before that, a copywriter.

At Goldsmiths, I am researching the history and cultural politics of inforamtion processes, notably compression and encryption. I have taught courses in digital art and culture, media theory, cultural politics and film studies. I've published and presented work on JPEG, psycho-visual models, the digital commodity form, Stuart Hall's encoding/decoding model and the politics of idleness.

Within the Autonomy Institute's Policy Studio, I work on progressive policy proposals for work, welfare and the climate.
