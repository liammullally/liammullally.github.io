Liam Mullally

I am a researcher, lecturer and theorist with a PhD in Cultural Studies from Goldsmiths University of London. I remain affiliated to Goldsmiths as a Graduate School Fellow, and hold the position of Researcher at the Autonomy Institute - a progressive policy research organisation. I have almost a decade of professional writing experience, spanning commercial, policy and academic forms.

As an academic researcher, my work falls primarily in the fields of software studies and media studies, with a particular interest in networked communication technologies. My PhD posed an interdisciplinary investigation of two pivotal information processes - compression and encryption - drawing links between the technical, the cultural, the political and the economic.

In the field of public policy, I research and develop proposals for the future of work, unconditionality and universal basic services (UBS). I develop and pilot innovative solutions to expand the possibilities of public provision on a common or universal basis.
